# OMNIABASE — LEXICON

## Status

This document defines the shared vocabulary of the Omniabase framework.

Its purpose is to provide a stable conceptual language across branches, repositories, and implementations.

This is not a branch-specific glossary.
It is the common lexicon of the general framework.

---

## 1. Representation

A **representation** is any workable form in which a phenomenon can be rendered, encoded, described, projected, or formalized for structural analysis.

A representation may include, for example:

- a numerical base
- a coordinate system
- a symbolic encoding
- a linguistic description
- a data projection
- a sensory channel
- a formal model
- a discretization or transformation scheme

A representation is not assumed to be neutral.

It is treated as both:

- a carrier of structure
- a source of constraint and distortion

---

## 2. Coding

A **coding** is a specific implemented representational form used to express a phenomenon.

A coding is a concrete representational choice.

Examples:

- binary encoding
- decimal encoding
- tokenized text
- graph encoding
- time-series discretization
- coordinate projection
- symbolic formalization

In Omniabase, codings are the operational units through which cross-representational analysis becomes possible.

---

## 3. Recoding

**Recoding** is the act of rendering the same phenomenon into a different coding while preserving enough structural continuity for comparison.

Recoding is not arbitrary rewriting.

A valid recoding must remain structurally meaningful relative to the object under study.

Recoding is central to Omniabase because structural behavior becomes visible when the same object is compared across multiple codings.

---

## 4. Plural representation

**Plural representation** is the use of multiple simultaneous codings of the same phenomenon as a method of structural analysis.

In Omniabase, plural representation is not treated as redundancy.

It is treated as a controlled way to test:

- what remains stable
- what changes
- what emerges
- what collapses

Plural representation is one of the foundational moves of the framework.

---

## 5. Representation privilege

**Representation privilege** is the unjustified assumption that one chosen representation has priority strong enough to define the phenomenon exhaustively.

Examples of privileged representations include:

- one numerical base treated as natural by default
- one coordinate system treated as final
- one linguistic description treated as sufficient
- one observer perspective treated as absolute
- one sensor channel treated as definitive

Omniabase suspends representation privilege as a structural principle.

---

## 6. Representation dependence

**Representation dependence** refers to structural features that appear, disappear, or change substantially when the representation changes.

A property is representation-dependent when it cannot be treated as structurally stable across valid recodings.

Representation dependence is not an error by itself.

It is a measurable property of how a phenomenon behaves under recoding.

---

## 7. Representation-resistant structure

**Representation-resistant structure** is structure that remains legible, detectable, or functionally stable across multiple valid codings.

This does not require exact sameness in every view.

It requires structural persistence strong enough to survive controlled representational variation.

Representation-resistant structure is one of the main targets of Omniabase analysis.

---

## 8. Structural residue

A **structural residue** is what remains available for comparison after the same phenomenon has been observed across multiple codings.

A residue may be:

- shared
- partial
- degraded
- emergent
- incompatible
- invariant only within limits

Structural residue is not necessarily the whole phenomenon.

It is the comparably recoverable structural remainder across representations.

---

## 9. Invariant residue

An **invariant residue** is the component of structural residue that remains stable across a family of valid recodings.

This is one of the strongest forms of evidence available inside the Omniabase framework.

It does not imply metaphysical certainty.

It implies structural persistence under controlled representational change.

---

## 10. Divergence

**Divergence** is the measurable structural difference that appears when the same phenomenon is compared across codings.

Divergence may signal:

- fragility
- encoding sensitivity
- regime difference
- loss of structure
- incompatible descriptions
- hidden anisotropy

Divergence is not automatically failure.

It is a structural event requiring classification.

---

## 11. Emergence

**Emergence** refers to structure that becomes visible only when multiple codings are compared.

A feature is emergent, in the Omniabase sense, when it is not clearly accessible in one isolated representation but becomes legible through cross-representational analysis.

Emergence may include:

- hidden coordinates
- latent separations
- invisible symmetries
- structural anisotropies
- cross-view patterns

---

## 12. Collapse

**Collapse** is the loss of structural coherence under representational change.

A structure collapses when apparent regularity in one representation fails to survive recoding strongly enough to remain structurally meaningful.

Collapse may indicate:

- local artifact
- fragility
- poor coding adequacy
- regime instability
- false apparent stability

In Omniabase, collapse is a structural result, not a rhetorical judgment.

---

## 13. Compatibility

**Compatibility** is the degree to which different descriptions, codings, or formalisms preserve enough shared structure to be treated as related to the same object.

Compatibility does not require identity.

It requires recoverable structural continuity.

Compatibility is one of the main outputs of the translation branch of Omniabase.

---

## 14. Incompatibility

**Incompatibility** is the degree to which different descriptions fail to preserve sufficient shared structural residue.

Incompatibility may arise from:

- genuinely different objects
- destructive recoding
- mismatched granularity
- incompatible formalisms
- structural loss
- observer privilege hidden in one description

Incompatibility is not necessarily an error.
It may be the correct structural diagnosis.

---

## 15. Hidden coordinate

A **hidden coordinate** is a descriptive axis that becomes structurally useful only when the phenomenon is observed across multiple codings rather than a single privileged one.

A hidden coordinate is not merely a derived variable.

It is a variable whose relevance becomes legible through representational variation.

Hidden coordinates are central to the Coordinate Discovery branch.

---

## 16. Latent factor

A **latent factor** is a candidate structural driver inferred from recurring cross-representational patterns.

In Omniabase, a latent factor is not assumed from semantic theory first.
It is proposed from structural recurrence, separation, or stability patterns across codings.

Not every latent factor becomes a validated coordinate.

A latent factor is an intermediate structural hypothesis.

---

## 17. Structural map

A **structural map** is an organized representation of how a phenomenon distributes, separates, persists, or changes across codings.

Structural maps may display:

- clusters
- regime separations
- hidden axes
- compatibility gradients
- collapse zones
- stable residues

A structural map is an output form, not a branch by itself.

---

## 18. Cross-representation analysis

**Cross-representation analysis** is the comparison of the same phenomenon across multiple codings in order to study structural persistence, divergence, emergence, compatibility, and collapse.

This is the general operational mode of Omniabase.

It is broader than diagnostics, broader than coordinate discovery, and broader than translation.

It is the common analytical action across all branches.

---

## 19. Observer privilege

**Observer privilege** is the assumption that one observer, one sensor, one descriptive frame, or one human-default perspective has foundational authority over the phenomenon.

Omniabase rejects this as a default assumption.

This does not remove observers.
It removes their monopoly.

Observer privilege is especially important in the epistemic pre-layer linked to observer suspension.

---

## 20. Structural measurement

**Structural measurement** is the act of quantifying or comparing structural behavior across representations without collapsing the result into semantic explanation or decision.

Structural measurement may concern:

- persistence
- divergence
- compatibility
- fragility
- emergence
- collapse
- latent separation

Structural measurement belongs to the measurement layer only.

---

## 21. Interpretation

**Interpretation** is the domain-level assignment of meaning to structural results.

Examples:

- clinical interpretation
- mathematical interpretation
- model interpretation
- causal interpretation
- linguistic interpretation

Interpretation is necessary in applications, but it is not the same as structural measurement.

Omniabase requires these layers to remain distinct.

---

## 22. Decision

**Decision** is the action layer that follows measurement and interpretation.

Examples:

- trigger alert
- reject output
- escalate case
- select model
- reformulate representation
- intervene in a system

Decision is downstream of measurement and interpretation.

It must not be confused with either.

---

## 23. Branch

A **branch** is a canonical operational family derived from the Omniabase framework.

The three canonical branches are:

- Diagnostics
- Coordinate Discovery
- Cross-Representation Translation

A branch is not the same as a repository or implementation.

A branch is a stable conceptual family.

---

## 24. Pre-layer

A **pre-layer** is a conceptual gateway that prepares the transition into Omniabase without being identical to one of its canonical branches.

A pre-layer may:

- weaken observer privilege
- challenge single-view assumptions
- expose representational limitations
- prepare cross-representational thinking

`observer-suspension` is an example of an epistemic pre-layer.

---

## 25. Satellite repository

A **satellite repository** is a repository related to the Omniabase ecosystem without being identical to the general framework or one of its branch definitions.

A satellite may contain:

- historical context
- branch-specific experiments
- frozen theoretical notes
- applied case studies
- linked but non-canonical developments

Satellite repositories may be important, but they do not define the framework itself.

---

## 26. Framework boundary

The **framework boundary** marks what Omniabase does not directly claim to measure.

Omniabase does not directly measure:

- pure meaning in itself
- pure interiority in itself
- absolute causation in itself
- phenomena that leave no workable structural trace

Its boundary is defined by the availability of transformable, comparable structural residues.

---

## 27. Canonical formula

The shortest valid summary of the Omniabase lexicon is this:

a phenomenon is not exhausted by one view;
vary the representation, compare the structure, and study what remains, emerges, or fails.
