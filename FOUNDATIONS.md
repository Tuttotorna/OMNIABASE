# OMNIABASE — FOUNDATIONS

## Status

Foundational document for the general Omniabase framework.

This file defines the core premises, the epistemic scope, and the non-negotiable structural principles of Omniabase.

It does not define a single implementation.
It defines the common ground from which all Omniabase branches derive.

---

## 1. First premise

A phenomenon should not be treated as exhausted by a single representation.

A coordinate system, a numerical base, a symbolic encoding, a linguistic description, a sensory interface, or a chosen formalism may be sufficient for local use, but not sufficient to determine which aspects belong to the phenomenon itself and which aspects belong only to the adopted view.

Omniabase begins from this limitation.

---

## 2. Representation is not neutral

A representation is not only a container of information.

It is also a constraint, a filter, a distortion channel, and a privilege structure.

What appears stable in one representation may disappear in another.
What appears invisible in one representation may emerge in another.
What appears simple in one representation may reveal hidden structure under recoding.

For this reason, representation itself must become part of the analysis.

---

## 3. Plural representation as method

Omniabase treats plural representation as a method, not as a secondary variation.

The same phenomenon can be observed across multiple simultaneous codings in order to detect:

- what remains stable
- what changes
- what emerges
- what collapses
- what depends on the chosen encoding
- what survives beyond it

This is not redundancy.
It is structural interrogation.

---

## 4. Core operational principle

Omniabase studies structure through variation of representation.

Its central operation is not merely to re-encode the same object many times, but to compare the structural behavior of that object across multiple codings.

The goal is to expose:

- invariance
- divergence
- emergence
- fragility
- compatibility
- hidden coordinates

A phenomenon becomes more legible when representation is made variable and analyzable.

---

## 5. Structural realism of Omniabase

Omniabase does not claim direct access to the thing-in-itself.

Its working realism is operational:

that which remains stable, emerges consistently, or collapses reproducibly under controlled variation of representation belongs to the structural behavior of the phenomenon more strongly than what appears only inside a single privileged view.

This is not metaphysical certainty.
It is structural evidence.

---

## 6. What Omniabase measures

Omniabase can be used to measure, depending on the branch:

- representation-resistant structure
- representation-dependent structure
- cross-view emergence
- hidden coordinates
- structural fragility
- regime separation
- cross-representation compatibility
- residual invariant structure

It is therefore not tied to one domain, one metric, or one application family.

---

## 7. What Omniabase does not assume

Omniabase does not assume:

- that all representations are equivalent
- that every recoding is valid
- that plural representation automatically yields truth
- that structure is always recoverable
- that semantics can be replaced by representation analysis
- that every phenomenon can be fully reconstructed through coding alone

Its claim is narrower and stronger:

representation variation can reveal structural properties that single-view analysis systematically misses.

---

## 8. Observer privilege

A privileged observer is not treated as the foundation of the phenomenon.

Human-centered description, standard coordinates, dominant numerical systems, linguistic default frames, and single-sensor perspectives are all treated as local conveniences, not absolute anchors.

From the Omniabase perspective, a privileged observer is only one possible view among others.

This does not eliminate observation.
It removes its monopoly.

---

## 9. Separation principle

Omniabase requires a structural separation between:

- measurement
- interpretation
- decision

Measurement asks:
what remains, emerges, diverges, or collapses across representations?

Interpretation asks:
what does this mean inside a model or domain?

Decision asks:
what action should follow?

These layers must not be collapsed into one.

This separation is non-negotiable.

---

## 10. General scope

Omniabase applies wherever a phenomenon can be rendered into multiple workable codings, encodings, projections, or descriptive views.

This may include:

- dynamical systems
- symbolic sequences
- model outputs
- sensor streams
- mathematical objects
- formal descriptions
- multimodal correspondences
- human-AI structural interfaces

The unifying condition is not the domain.
The unifying condition is the existence of cross-representational structure.

---

## 11. Boundary of the framework

Omniabase does not cover what leaves no workable trace, no transformable structure, and no comparable residue.

It does not directly measure pure meaning, pure interiority, or absolute causation in themselves.

It operates on structural traces, not on metaphysical guarantees.

Its boundary is not a weakness.
It is what keeps the framework coherent.

---

## 12. Canonical consequences

From the core Omniabase principle, three canonical branches follow:

- Diagnostics
- Coordinate Discovery
- Cross-Representation Translation

These are not separate inventions.
They are three consequences of the same foundational move:

removing the privilege of a single representation.

---

## 13. Foundational formula

Omniabase begins from a simple statement:

a phenomenon is not exhausted by one view.

Its method is equally simple:

vary the representation, compare the structure, and study what remains, emerges, or fails.

That is the foundation of the framework.
