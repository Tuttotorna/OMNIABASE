# OMNIABASE — ARCHITECTURE

## Status

This document defines the architectural structure of the Omniabase ecosystem.

Its role is to separate the general framework from its derived branches, implementations, and satellite repositories.

---

## 1. Architectural principle

Omniabase is the general framework.

It is not a single implementation, not a single metric family, and not a single application domain.

It defines the common structural logic from which multiple branches can be derived.

The ecosystem must therefore be read in layers.

---

## 2. Layered structure

### Layer 0 — Foundational Principle

This is Omniabase at its most general level.

Core statement:

a phenomenon should not be treated as exhausted by a single representation.

At this level, Omniabase defines:

- the role of plural representation
- the structural importance of recoding
- the distinction between representation-dependent and representation-resistant structure
- the separation of measurement, interpretation, and decision

Repository role:

- `OMNIABASE`

---

### Layer 1 — Canonical Branches

From the foundational principle, three canonical branches follow.

#### A. Omniabase Diagnostics

Purpose:

measure whether observed structure remains stable beyond a single representation.

Central outputs:

- robustness
- fragility
- drift
- instability
- collapse proximity
- representation dependence

Typical role:

defensive, monitoring, post-hoc auditing, structural sensing.

Primary repository lineage:

- `OMNIA`
- `lon-mirror`
- `omnia-limit`
- `Pre-Deployment-Structural-Gate`
- `OMNIA-RADAR`

---

#### B. Omniabase Coordinate Discovery

Purpose:

use multiple representations to expose hidden axes, latent variables, regime separations, and more faithful structural coordinates.

Central outputs:

- hidden coordinates
- latent factors
- structural maps
- regime separations
- generator distinctions
- descriptive axes for modeling

Typical role:

generative, exploratory, scientific, model-building.

Primary repository lineage:

- `omniabase-coordinate-discovery`

---

#### C. Omniabase Cross-Representation Translation

Purpose:

measure compatibility and shared structural residue across different descriptions of the same object.

Central outputs:

- compatibility scores
- alignment measures
- invariant residues
- translatability maps
- incompatibility signals
- bridges across formalisms

Typical role:

interface, translation, alignment, model-to-model comparison.

Primary repository lineage:

- `omega-translator`
- `HASC-Human-AI-Structural-Compatibility-Protocol`
- future dedicated Omniabase translation branch if formalized separately

---

## 3. Epistemic pre-layer

Some repositories do not belong to a canonical branch directly, but act as epistemic pre-layers or conceptual precursors.

### Observer Suspension

Role:

remove the monopoly of a privileged observer and prepare the transition from observer-centered description to representation-variable analysis.

Function inside the ecosystem:

- epistemic decentering
- critique of observer privilege
- conceptual preparation for multirepresentational analysis

Primary repository:

- `observer-suspension`

This is not the whole of Omniabase.
It is one of its strongest conceptual gateways.

---

## 4. Adjacent and historical layers

Some repositories contain important related material, but should not be confused with the general Omniabase framework itself.

These may include:

- identity or origin documents
- frozen theoretical notes
- domain-specific case studies
- historical bridges to MB-X.01 / Omega / Dual-Echo lines
- early exploratory or experimental satellites

Their role is contextual, not foundational.

They may inform Omniabase, but they do not define the architecture of the framework.

---

## 5. Non-negotiable separations

The Omniabase ecosystem must preserve the following separations.

### A. Framework != Branch
The general framework is not identical to any one branch.

### B. Branch != Implementation
A branch may have multiple implementations, experiments, or repos.

### C. Measurement != Interpretation
Structural sensing is not the same as semantic explanation.

### D. Interpretation != Decision
Meaning attribution is not the same as action selection.

### E. Pre-layer != Main branch
Conceptual gateways such as observer suspension must not be confused with the full framework.

These separations are required for architectural clarity.

---

## 6. Role of the main repository

The `OMNIABASE` repository is the umbrella repository of the framework.

Its role is to host:

- the foundational principle
- the common language
- the architectural taxonomy
- the branch definitions
- the map of the ecosystem
- the links to derived branches and implementations

It is not the primary place for branch-specific benchmarks, code, or experimental claims.

Those belong in branch repositories.

---

## 7. Expected ecosystem shape

The intended ecosystem shape is:

- `OMNIABASE` -> general framework
- Diagnostics branch -> specialized repositories
- Coordinate Discovery branch -> specialized repositories
- Cross-Representation Translation branch -> specialized repositories
- epistemic pre-layers and satellites -> separate but linked

This preserves both unity and specialization.

---

## 8. Architectural summary

Omniabase is the general multirepresentational framework.

Its current architecture can be read as:

- foundation -> `OMNIABASE`
- defensive branch -> Diagnostics
- generative branch -> Coordinate Discovery
- interface branch -> Cross-Representation Translation
- epistemic gateway -> Observer Suspension

This architecture is intended to remain stable even as implementations evolve.
